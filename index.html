about

The fifth edition of Kyiv Biennial will be international and will take place in Kyiv,
Ivano-Frankivsk, Uzhgorod, Berlin, Vienna and Warsaw.

With a cascade openings the Biennial will kick-off in October in Kyiv and will finish the year of 2023 in Berlin. The upcoming biennial aims at reintegrating the Ukrainian artistic community, divided and scattered throughout Europe by the war, to empower its actors to work and reflect collectively on cultural, social and environmental challenges Ukraine is currently facing, installing it into a global context. Kyiv Biennial 2023 will be conceived as a pan-European event, with dispersed exhibitions and public programs in a number of Ukrainian and EU cities, partnering with leading European institutions in the field of contemporary art.

In view of the brutal Russian attack on Ukraine, the possibility of a comprehensive biennial project in Kyiv long seemed more than uncertain, if not impossible. How can a country at war address political, social, cultural and societal issues? Today, the experience of artists and cultural workers in Ukraine is profoundly marked by war trauma, displacement, lack of access to basic resources, and, in many cases, life under military occupation, or direct involvement in armed resistance. This poses existential challenges for the future of art and cultural production in Ukraine, as its institutional infrastructure is getting physically erased.

Instead of abandoning the project and thus submitting to the logic of war that attacks everything civil, the Biennial recalled its founding idea of being a multi-centric initiative and mobilized cultural institutions in Europe to host the 2023 edition in a European, interconnected, and solidary form. Art institutions in Ukraine, in Kyiv, Ivano-Frankivsk and Uzhgorod organize presentations and events in their endangered yet working infrastructures. Museums and exhibition halls in Warsaw, Berlin and Vienna and other European cities have, despite the late start of planning due to the war, freed up their spaces and platforms for exhibitions and events with Ukrainian and international artists, as well as for discursive, performative and educational activities. Together, the institutions form a curatorial consortium to jointly create a conceptual framework within which they develop their respective Kyiv Biennial programs.

Kyiv Biennial strategy, which was developed in the course of the four of its previous editions, merges artistic production, critical knowledge and social engagement in the times of emergency, where curatorship goes much beyond its contemporary meaning of an artistic and organizational practice and becomes resignified with its original sense of restoration, rehabilitation and relief, thus suggesting not a biennial, but a perennial long-run strategy reaching out to East Europe's devastated lands. The upcoming biennial aims at reintegrating the Ukrainian artistic community, divided and scattered throughout Europe by the war, to empower its actors to work and reflect collectively on cultural, social and environmental challenges Ukraine is currently facing, installing it into a global context.

Kyiv Biennial 2023 is conceived and organized by the Visual Culture Research Center together with tranzit.at, Museum of Modern Art in Warsaw, Other Edges, Dovzhenko Center , Asortymentna Kimnata (Ivano Frankivsk) and Sorry, No Rooms Available.
